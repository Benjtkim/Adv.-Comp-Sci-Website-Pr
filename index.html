What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. Cyber attacks are most commonly used to access, change, or destroy sensitive information; extort money with the use of malware; or interfere with usual business operations. Effective cybersecurity will become more challenging as time passes and attackers become more innovative.

Types of cybersecurity threats

Not all cyber attacks are grandiose. A threat as simple as phishing (sending fraudulent emails that resemble communications from reputable sources) is considered a cyber attack. Usually, these emails aim to rob sensitive information such as credit card numbers and login data. Since phishing is the most common kind of cyber attack, there are multiple solutions that ward against it by filtering malicious emails.

Malware is any software that’s designed to damage or gain unauthorized access to a computer. Phishing emails are dangerous precisely because of it since they usually function by tricking the user into clicking a link that leads to the installation of malware. If the installation were allowed to finish and/or the program allowed to run, malicious code executed by the software could carry out any of a variety of unanticipated actions such as:
  • Filling up the screen or browser with ads,
  • destroying essential system components to render the computer unusable,
  • preventing the access of files, other programs, or maybe even the computer itself,
  • or downloading other software that records what’s typed or consumes system resources, often operating without the user’s knowledge and slowing down the system significantly.
Some malware may even copy themselves into parts of the file system they weren’t downloaded in, making them more difficult to remove. Malware may use any number of different tactics to cause the user to trigger it to run, but the most common trigger is a click, usually on a pop-up created by the software or a link.

Ransomware is a specific type of malware designed to extort money by preventing the user from accessing their files or the computer itself until a ransom is paid. It should be noted that paying the ransom is no guarantee the lost files will be restored or the system returned.

How cybersecurity protects your data

Firewalls function as barriers that filter incoming network traffic from a source such as the internet or otherwise. A firewall analyzes which network traffic to accept or not based on the pre-existing rules it’s installed with, meaning it only welcomes connections it had been configured to accept.

Antivirus products detect, quarantine, and/or delete suspicious code in an attempt to prevent malware from infecting your device by constantly scanning it for threats. If one is found, the product should prevent you from accessing that suspicious file or website, and attempt to eliminate the threat. Antivirus software may use any number of the following methods to detect and remove viruses and malware: signature-based analysis, heuristic analysis, sandbox detection, artificial intelligence, and behavior monitoring.

Infamous Cyber Attacks

Shamoon was a virus that infected the computers of the state-owned, national-oil company "Saudi Aramco" when an inisder with privileged access unleashed it onto the network on August, 2012. Designed to render any computers it came into contact with useless, Shamoon erased data from countless hard drives and replaced them with images of a burning American flag. Shamoon mostly wiped data by rewriting systems with garbage information. It is believed that terroristic hacking groups within Iran were responsible for the attack, but evidence to support that claim is few and far between, primarily since U.S. officials have declined to disclose what material they've got on the subject.

Heartland Payement Systems suffered an enormous data breach when two Russian hackers gained access to the company's corporate network by way of exploiting SQL vulnerabilites and antiquated code that written in 2002. It's estimated they robbed nearly 100 million debit and credit card numbers.
